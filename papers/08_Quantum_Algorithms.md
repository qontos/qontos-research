## Abstract

This paper defines the QONTOS algorithm roadmap for modular fault-tolerant quantum systems. It establishes a benchmark ladder from small chemistry and optimization problems toward a stretch-case FeMoco flagship target. The paper treats FeMoco as a long-term benchmark because it is scientifically meaningful and commercially resonant, while emphasizing that algorithmic readiness must be built progressively through increasingly demanding workload classes.

**Claim status:** algorithm roadmap paper with benchmark-ladder framing

---

## 1. Why Algorithms Must Be Framed as a Ladder

Large-scale algorithm claims are most credible when they sit on top of smaller validated workloads. QONTOS therefore presents FeMoco as the top of a benchmark ladder with clear intermediate progression.

### 1.1 Claim Status Summary

| Claim | Status |
|---|---|
| FeMoco is a strong flagship benchmark | Derived from literature and strategy |
| FeMoco remains the stretch benchmark for the 2030 roadmap | Stretch target |

## 3. FeMoco as the Stretch Flagship

### 3.1 Why FeMoco

FeMoco remains attractive because it is:

- scientifically important
- widely recognized as a quantum benchmark
- linked to compelling industrial narratives

### 3.2 Correct Framing

FeMoco should be described as:

- a flagship stretch benchmark
- a long-term target
- an anchor for resource-estimation and benchmark methodology

It fits best as the flagship stretch benchmark that guides the long-range architecture and resource-estimation program.

### 3.3 Resource-Estimation Posture

Any qubit, gate-count, runtime, or error-rate table should be explicitly labeled as:

- literature-derived
- simulator-estimated
- digital-twin-estimated
- QONTOS target

| Scenario | What is technically realistic to discuss |
|---|---|
| Base | small chemistry and optimization benchmarks on the current platform and digital twin |
| Aggressive | medium modular workloads and early fault-tolerant benchmark progression |

### 4.1 Safe Public Message

`QONTOS uses FeMoco as a long-term benchmark target while progressing through a staged ladder of smaller chemistry, optimization, and modular benchmark workloads.`

---

## 5. Validation Gates

The algorithm story should only move upward when these are satisfied:

1. smaller benchmark classes run reproducibly
2. resource-estimation methodology is clearly documented
3. benchmark ladder stages are tied to architecture and QEC assumptions
4. flagship algorithm claims are only made after intermediate milestones are passed

---

## 6. Conclusion

This paper is strongest when it treats algorithms as the final proof layer of the QONTOS architecture rather than as a standalone vision statement. The technically feasible narrative is:

- QONTOS has a benchmark ladder
- FeMoco remains the stretch flagship
- progress should be measured through staged workload classes, not one heroic endpoint
